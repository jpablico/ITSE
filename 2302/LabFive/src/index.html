Owls of North Texas

North Texas is home to a variety of owl species. Here are some of the most common owls you might see in the region:

Common Name Scientific Name Habitat Diet
Eastern Screech Owl Megascops asio Woodlands Insects, small mammals
Barred Owl Strix varia Swamps, dense forests Fish, small mammals, birds
Great Horned Owl Bubo virginianus Forests, prairies, deserts Rodents, birds, reptiles
Barn Owl Tyto alba Open country, farms, urban areas Rodents, small mammals
Short-eared Owl Asio flammeus Grasslands, marshes Small mammals, birds
Long-eared Owl Asio otus Forests, woodlands Small mammals, birds
Northern Saw-whet Owl Aegolius acadicus Forests, woodlands Small mammals, birds
Elf Owl Micrathene whitneyi Deserts, arid regions Insects, scorpions
Flammulated Owl Psiloscops flammeolus Forests, woodlands Insects
Western Screech Owl Megascops kennicottii Woodlands, deserts Insects, small mammals
Boreal Owl Aegolius funereus Coniferous forests Small mammals, birds
Spotted Owl Strix occidentalis Dense old-growth forests Small mammals, birds
Whiskered Screech Owl Megascops trichopsis Mountainous woodlands Insects, small mammals
Ferruginous Pygmy Owl Glaucidium brasilianum Woodlands, open areas Insects, small mammals, birds
Stygian Owl Asio stygius Forests, dense woodlands Birds, small mammals
Mexican Spotted Owl Strix occidentalis lucida Canyons, dense forests Small mammals, birds
Burrowing Owl Athene cunicularia Open grasslands, deserts Insects, small mammals
Eastern Barn Owl Tyto alba pratincola Open country, farmland Small mammals, birds
Snowy Owl Bubo scandiacus Open tundra, fields Rodents, birds
Hawk Owl Surnia ulula Coniferous forests, open woodlands Small mammals, birds
